Glossary
Every metric on this dashboard, explained. Formulas show how each number is calculated.
Full Target
The cumulative price total a stock must reach across the entire FCA review period to graduate. Set by OJK regulation — it's the benchmark, not a prediction.
Full Target = Total Trading Days × 51
Example: 62 trading days × 51 = 3,162 points needed to graduate
Current Total
The running sum of all daily closing prices since the FCA period started. This is where the stock actually stands right now — the accumulated score.
Current Total = Σ (Close Price for each elapsed trading day)
Example: Day 1 close 80 + Day 2 close 82 + Day 3 close 79 = 241
Shortfall
How far the stock still needs to climb. The gap between where it needs to be (Full Target) and where it actually is (Current Total). A negative number means it's already past the target.
Shortfall = Full Target − Current Total
Example: 3,162 target − 500 current = 2,662 shortfall
Avg Needed
The average closing price the stock needs for each remaining session to hit the Full Target. Think of it as the required run rate — what the stock must average per day from here.
Avg Needed = Shortfall ÷ Remaining Sessions
Example: 2,662 shortfall ÷ 61 sessions = 43.6 per day needed
Remaining Sessions
The number of trading days left in the FCA review period, excluding weekends and IDX holidays. Each remaining session is a chance to close the gap.
Remaining Sessions = Total Trading Days − Elapsed Trading Days
Example: 62 total − 1 elapsed = 61 sessions remaining
Daily Closes
The end-of-day closing price for each trading day in the review period. These are the raw data points — every number on this dashboard is built from daily closes.
Daily Close = Last traded price of the day on IDX
Example: KBAG closed at Rp50 on May 29 → that's one daily close added to the cumulative total
Monthly Breakdown
A month-by-month view of how the cumulative total is building up. Shows points earned per month vs. the monthly target, and whether each month was above or below pace. The Δ column shows surplus (+) or deficit (−) for that month.
Monthly Target = Days in Month × 51 | Δ = Monthly Points − Monthly Target
Example: May had 1 trading day → target 51. Stock earned 50 → Δ = −1
Progress to Target
How far along the stock is toward the Full Target, shown as a percentage. 100% means it's already graduated. Below 50% means there's more ground to cover than ground covered.
Progress = (Current Total ÷ Full Target) × 100%
Example: 500 current ÷ 3,162 target = 15.8%
Scenarios
What-if projections showing whether the stock can graduate under different conditions. Each scenario assumes a specific price trajectory for the remaining sessions and calculates the resulting total.
Scenario Total = Current Total + (Assumed Price × Remaining Sessions)
Example: Status quo assumes current price for all remaining sessions; rally assumes capped ARA run
Verdict
A plain-language assessment of graduation likelihood based on the gap between Avg Needed and the stock's current price. Only shown after 10+ trading days of data.
Verdict thresholds: ON TRACK ≤ current price | TIGHT ≤ 1.1× | UNLIKELY ≤ 1.3× | IMPOSSIBLE > 1.3×
Example: Avg Needed is 43.6, current price is 50 → ON TRACK
Pace Needed / Day
The primary metric in early phase — how much each remaining trading day needs to average for graduation. Compared against current price to gauge feasibility.
Pace = Shortfall ÷ Remaining Sessions
Example: Need Rp43.6/day, current price Rp50 → pace is achievable
